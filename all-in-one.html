All in ONE

  • UX/UI designer
  • Case Study - CareerFoundry Project
  • Mobile and Ipad
  • Duration: 8 Months
  • Tools: Figma,Miro,Illustator.

All in One is a financial platform that consolidates all your accounts in one place, ensuring you never miss a beat. It allows make single or multiple payments to accounts/mobile phones, create saving pots and track transaction history.

Misplacing your wallet will be a worry of the past, with our cutting-edge contactless payment feature.

Problem Statement

Our users need a way to be able to pay, transfer and control their money without the need for physical cards - all these tasks condensed in 1 app.

Because in this way they will be able to be more organised, feel under control with their money, and be more efficient and satisfied when actioning overwhelming financial tasks.

Empathise

Identifying the gaps, needs, and pain points that users in the UK face when using financial apps, in order to understand their current struggles and find opportunities that can improve their life's.

Competitive analysis

This was made to understand what is on the market, what was needed to be done in a different way, what are the current positions of my competitors and what could be improved with my new solution.

I applied a S.W.O.T and UX analysis onto 2 of the most recognised banking apps - Monzo and Revolut- that allow people to make payments, transfers, shopping online and manage their finances. Finding their strengths, weakness, opportunities and threads to understand what users currently use and their difficulties.

Research Goals

  1. Understand how users behave when doing shopping, transfers, and paying online.
  2. Understand what are the users' interests in similar apps.
  3. To identify what are their struggles when normally doing these tasks

Qualitative research

I applied qualitative research based on observation, surveys and interviews to gain more in-depth insights of the current situation that people face when using financial apps. In order to understand better the needs and frustrations when using these apps. Allowing me to start defining my audience and also potential problems that need solving.

Key insights derived

  • Current banking/financial app users keep using the apps from their actual bank, because they don not know about other alternative apps and how they work without signing up.
  • According to users, the most popular apps require you to create an account, but they do not exactly explain how it works prior signing up.
  • Users struggle and get frustrated when managing their finances, because have different apps for different tasks.
  • They also struggle when wanting to do fast payments to split bills.
  • In the competitor and UX analysis for Monzo and Revolut apps, I found out that they contain great features for managing their finances and make payments, but require the user to open another bank account in order to be able to use the app. They also do not exactly explain how they work prior to signing up.
  • In addition to the above, the majority of their features are too advanced for people who are only interested in managing and doing their “traditional” everyday financial transactions, such as paying, shopping, transferring money, or saving.

Define

Based on the above research, I applied the Double Diamond strategy to define and create the problem statement (please see above), hypothesis and defined the path of the project.

This stage was also used to define my users, their needs and struggles when using apps to manage their finances.

Hypothesis

Our users need a way to be able to pay, transfer and control their money without the need for physical cards - all these tasks condensed in 1 app.

We will know this to be true when we see high usage rates of the app, an increase of users, receive successful reviews and see people recommending the app to friends and family.

BRD

To keep the team informed about the direction of the project, I created a BRD taking into account: Target audience, market analysis, risk opportunity and SMART business objectives.

Key insights

We have a strong gap in the market if we create an app that will allow to pay, transfer, manage and keep an eye to all the accounts, without the need to exit and open onto another app to control finances, transfer money or see different bank accounts.

I created my personas to determine what our users want to accomplish this is done with surveys, research goals and interviews to get know better my target audience.

Following by a card sorting and affinity mapping that will determine my 2 personas Roxanne and Robert.

Key insights derived

  • The majority of interviewers use different(individual) apps for transfers, do shopping and payments.
  • The rewards program is not actioned much as interviewers are unaware of how to use it.
  • There is an important need for practicality and the idea of having these 3 tasks condensed in 1 place caught their attention.
  • They are very worried about their money and the safety of their details when using financial apps.
  • Users still prefer shopping in actual brand websites/stores as it can guarantee them the reliability of the page.
  • Contactless is what they now use to pay nowadays.
  • The last task that interests them is transfers, but they still use it occasionally.

Ideate

Applying brain storming techniques in order to create alternatives and innovative solutions for the personas. Furthermore, I applied user flows, information architecture and mobile first design approach to start organising users navigation and app structure solutions.

User flow

After applying card sorting to take into account all the components of the app. The user flows were applied to create the navigation of the app taking into account our personas goals and innovative solutions to a smooth and happy path.

Top solutions

  • Onboarding- explaining the main features of the app.
  • Incorporating single and multiple payments.
  • Adding an option to send money to mobile phones.
  • Making transactions navigation similar to any other bank account so our users do not get confused when transitioning to our app.
  • Being able to pay contactless.
  • Allowed to have savings in a separate place from the accounts.
  • Being able to have multiple bank accounts in the same app with the possibility of managing it.
  • Confirming transactions to provide final success on the task and bringing satisfaction to our users.
  • Entry point: Open App
  • Success criteria: Confirmation of transactions

Test

The testing was done moderated remote and in person to identify pain points where to iterate and enhance the usability.

Test objectives

  • How to add cards to the wallet.
  • See how participants make payments.
  • See how participants to add/remove/withdraw saving pots.
  • How to filter transaction history.

Key insights derived

Our participants were very excited about the usability of the app despite the issues that require iteration. They also liked the aesthetics and colours of it. Nevertheless, it was well taken into account that darker colours gave them the feeling the app was more for crypto.

Iteration

Latest results of iteration taking into account our personas and latest findings from the the usability test. Ideally to improve and enhance the app for an smoother navigation.

Insights derived

The app will require another usability test to ensure all our issues have been covered, and if needed pass to another process of iteration and improvement after testing.

As an overall, we have included and solved pain points and struggles that we found from the empathise process and brought them a solution in order to bring success to the app, but mainly to satisfy our users and hopefully welcome new interested users.

Ta-dah!

UI Style: Modern, simple, playful

UI Style Guide

By designing and researching in All in One it was discovered that the app can efficiently satisfy personas that still are struggling with current apps.

Solving struggles that apps currently in the market have not been able to solve, giving a business opportunity and solution for customers.

During the research process, it was a struggle to arrange interviews and surveys and was time taking which extended the project due to delays in re-arranging.

Key learning

Each stage of our design process plays a critical role in guaranteeing the success of the project. Sometimes is also better to test the project as it is and receive feedback at early stages - if prototypes allow.

Designers need to be aware of what can be developed by the team otherwise the outcome will have to change.

All in One will require another usability test that will provide further feedback to the app
